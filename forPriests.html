For Priests

Priests today face many challenges. Loneliness, a busy schedule and stress can become overwhelming, leading to health problems, a diminished life of prayer and a general feeling of discontent in ministry. The mutual support offered by living in community while pursuing common goals of holiness and service of Christ and His Church helps priests to live and minster in a more peaceful, balanced way. We priests of the Society can attest to the great blessing that our community life has brought to us and to our ministry. While currently a Private Clerical Association of the Christian Faithful, we aim – if God wills it – to become in time a Society of Apostolic Life. Rather than pursuing a model of priesthood more fitting to an Institute of Consecrated Life (such as sharing goods in common), the Society of St. Leo the Great is firmly grounded in a truly diocesan spirituality. Our model of priestly and community life helps us to serve God in the assignments that we receive from our respective bishops. Men already serving as diocesan priests and who are interested in learning more about living in community according to a common way of life are encouraged to contact us with the link provided.
